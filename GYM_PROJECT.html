Political Parties

BJP

The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) is a prominent right-leaning political party in India. Founded in 1980, it emerged from the Hindu nationalist Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) and has a core ideology rooted in Hindutva, emphasizing Hindu culture and values. Under the leadership of Narendra Modi, the BJP achieved electoral success, winning the 2014 and 2019 general elections, forming the government in India. It has pursued economic reforms, infrastructure development, and nationalist policies. However, the BJP has also faced criticism for religious tensions and polarization. It remains a dominant force in Indian politics, shaping the country's trajectory. Read More

CONGRESS

The Congress and BJP are two major political parties in India. The Indian National Congress, founded in 1885, has a center-left ideology and played a pivotal role in India's struggle for independence. It has a history of social and economic policies. The Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP), founded in 1980, leans right, with roots in Hindu nationalism. Under Narendra Modi's leadership, the BJP has gained prominence, focusing on economic development, nationalism, and cultural preservation. These two parties have been primary rivals in Indian politics, with the Congress seeking to uphold secularism and the BJP emphasizing Hindutva, often leading to political and ideological clashes. NOTA

The "NOTA" (None of the Above) option in elections allows voters to express their dissatisfaction with all available candidates. When selected on a ballot, it signifies that the voter does not support any candidate and wishes for better choices. NOTA was introduced in India in 2013 as a democratic expression of disapproval.
